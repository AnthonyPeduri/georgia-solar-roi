Why Georgia Solar Math Is Different

🔄

No True Net Metering

Georgia Power uses the RNR Solar Buy Back program, crediting excess solar at ~7.2¢/kWh — far below retail. States like NC credit at full retail (~12¢/kWh), making their payback 2–4 years shorter.

📊

Self-Consumption Is King

Because the export rate is low, running appliances during peak solar hours (10am–3pm) dramatically improves ROI. Every kWh you use directly saves you the full retail rate instead of the 7.2¢ export credit.

🎫

The 5,000-Spot Cap

Georgia Power's Solar Buy Back is capped at 5,000 participants statewide in a state of 11 million people. If the cap fills, new solar customers can't sell back to the grid at any rate.

💵

Federal ITC Still Stacks

The 30% federal ITC (2026, Section 25D) applies regardless of your utility's export rate. On a $24,000 system, that's $7,200 off your taxes — a dollar-for-dollar credit that significantly shortens payback.

Georgia Solar FAQ

Does Georgia have net metering?

Not in the traditional sense. Georgia Power offers the RNR Solar Buy Back program at approximately 7.2¢/kWh for 2026 — below the retail rate of 8–14¢/kWh. The program is also capped at 5,000 residential customers statewide.

What is the federal solar tax credit in 2026?

The residential federal ITC is 30% of total installed system cost under Section 25D. It's a dollar-for-dollar reduction in federal income taxes owed — not just a deduction. A $24,000 system generates a $7,200 tax credit.

What's the typical payback period in Georgia?

Due to the below-retail Solar Buy Back rate, Georgia payback periods typically run 9–13 years for a well-positioned system after the ITC. Most Georgia systems still have 12+ profitable years remaining after payback.

Is Sawnee EMC better for solar than Georgia Power?

Sawnee EMC (Forsyth, Dawson counties) has its own interconnection and buyback policies that may differ from Georgia Power. Some EMCs offer slightly more favorable export rates. Contact your specific EMC for their current solar buyback terms.

Does adding a battery improve ROI in Georgia?

Yes — primarily through bill savings and backup power value. A battery lets you store midday solar for evening use at retail savings rates instead of exporting at 7.2¢/kWh. In Georgia's low-export-rate environment, batteries make more financial sense than in net-metered states.

What's the maximum system size on Georgia Power?

Georgia Power's Solar Buy Back program caps residential systems at 10 kW AC. Systems larger than 10 kW can still be installed but won't qualify for the export credit program — all excess generation beyond your home's consumption would be wasted.
